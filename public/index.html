Station Eleven

by Emily St. John Mandel

Created by Lei0x1

Chapter 1:

  • On the night King Lear opens at the Elgin Theatre, Arthur Leander collapses in a heap halfway through the show. Jeevan, an audience member and paramedic in training, leaps onto the stage to try and save him. He starts CPR with the help of a cardiologist in the audience, but Arthur cannot be revived.

  • Jeevan comforts the young girl Kirsten and makes a resolution that he has to find her guardian. Closing the door, he feels guilt at having survived. On his way home, though in snow, he comes to a realization that he wants to be a paramedic.

Chapter 2:

  • At the Elgin Theatre, only a few were remaining behind. The woman was washing costumes, the man was doing some ironing, the actress was drinking tequila, the stagehand was mopping up the floor, while the caregiver was pacifying a whimpering child.

  • About Arthur's family, they talked in the lobby, finding out he had a son in Israel by the name of Tyler and several ex-wives. They decided to call his lawyer to cover his death.

  • They toasted to the absent Arthur and then disappeared into the snow. Backstage, Tanya gave Kirsten a glass paperweight with a storm cloud inside it. The bartender was the last one to leave and died three weeks later.

Chapter 3:

  • Jeevan walked around Allan Gardens in the hot house while the snow fell outside. He admired the plants and considered visiting his brother Frank to talk over the death of Arthur.

  • After leaving, he took a streetcar home, remembering Arthur and his friend Hua, who warned him about the Georgia Flu spreading fast.

  • Afraid for his life, Jeevan flees town. He stops at an ramen/grocery store to load up on supplies, and also tries calling Laura, his girlfriend, to warn her; she thinks he is overreacting. "You really ought to get out before it's too late," he said.

Chapter 4:

  • The only ones present in the Elgin Theatre that night were the security guard and the producer, who promptly called Arthur's lawyer with the news of his death. The latter spent two hours going over Arthur's will and promptly called Arthur's close friend at dawn, who in turn called Arthur's ex-wives that morning.

Chapter 5:

  • Working in Malaysia, Miranda received news that Arthur, the love of her life, died of a heart attack on stage during a play. It comes so ordinarily, leaving her to sort through the loss, to remember the truth that life goes on. Alone, she looks out at the ships far off, their lights illuminating across the waterways.

Chapter 6:

  • Everything from everyday objects is lost. There's no swimming, no night games, no cities. Movies and concerts are extremely seldom; medicines are non-existent. There's limited air travel; planes are used for storage. There are no more borders, no more emergency services. The internet's gone, so people cannot connect through the internet anymore.

Chapter 7:

  • Twenty years after air travel ended, the Traveling Symphony moved slowly through the heat near Lake Michigan. They walked to rest the horses and practiced lines from King Lear.

  • Kirsten remembered Arthur Leander, who gave her two special comics. She and August spoke about their past, the old technology that interested them. The Symphony continued to play their music and Shakespeare while journeying.

Chapter 8:

  • Kirsten has memorized two rare comics: Dr. Eleven, given to her by Arthur Leander. "They're about Dr. Eleven, a physicist on this beautiful space station." There were only ten copies, and they have become worn. One image shows him with this weird dog named Luli, contemplating Earth.

Chapter 9:

  • The Symphony makes it to St. Deborah, where they left Charlie pregnant. A guard there tells them to camp at the local Walmart, but there is nobody in the town. While rehearsing A Midsummer Night's Dream with her ex, Sayid, Kirsten becomes worried about Charlie.

Chapter 10:

  • There's some tension within the ranks with the Traveling Symphony, but love of music ultimately keeps them together. Kirsten searches for her friend Charlie and learns Charlie has left town with her family. She sees grave markers but finds they have escaped. The Symphony hears of an epidemic and changes in leadership of the town.

Chapter 11:

  • In a post-apocalyptic town, Kirsten performs as Titania in A Midsummer Night’s Dream. The Traveling Symphony values art and carries the motto “Because survival is not enough.”

Chapter 12:

  • After Kirsten's show, a man remembered as the prophet praises the Symphony and speaks on future dangers due to the flu. The conductor hears word of new rules leaving town. While leaving the town, the group discusses what risk lies ahead, while Kirsten reflects on memories of the past.

Chapter 13:

  • In a Toronto restaurant, actor Arthur Leander meets Miranda, a young woman leaving her boyfriend. He offers her a place to stay, sparking questions about their relationship.

  • As Arthur thinks about his fame, he reconnects with Miranda, now an art student, showing their different lives and true connection.

Chapter 14:

  • Miranda is an admin assistant in Neptune Logistics, who views herself as frumpy next to her stylish friend, Thea. Unhappy with her boyfriend, Pablo, Miranda meets for dinner with Arthur, an old friend and, in the process of breaking up with Pablo, starts an amour with him.

Chapter 15:

  • Miranda is upset about her failing marriage to Arthur after seeing him with another woman, Elizabeth. She considers leaving but faces her feelings instead.

  • She later feels alienated at home and finds Elizabeth drunk. Indeed, a photographer makes her feel vulnerable, but she warms up with Elizabeth, and they chat. Miranda more or less accepts her divorce and considers her future.

Chapter 16:

  • Francois Diallo interviews Kirsten Raymonde twenty-six years after Miranda and Arthur's last dinner party. Diallo started a newspaper and is seeking local stories since the majority of the population does not travel any more. Raymonde mentions some comics she showed him, which were given to her from Arthur Leander.

Chapter 17:

  • One year before the outbreak of the Georgia Flu epidemic, Clark was having dinner with his longtime friend Arthur in London. The way to the restaurant was a hassle for Clark, and after he finally arrived, he found that instead of talking with him, Arthur was interested in putting on a show. This made Clark feel so disappointed because he remembered how much their relationship had gone through since their days.

Chapter 18:

  • In an interview, Raymonde discusses her early acting career and memory issues from the collapse. She joined the Symphony at fourteen after losing her brother and describes a failed expedition to Ohio. Raymonde notes that while some towns are organized, others are chaotic or ruled by cults, leading to her frustration over their illogical beliefs and memories of lost family.

Chapter 19:

  • The Traveling Symphony often found themselves torn between the risky mission of keeping art alive and well. After finding a stowaway, Eleanor, who had run away from a prophet looking for a wife, they debated returning her or risking charges of kidnapping. Eleanor did name the Museum of Civilization as a possible destination; the fear was it could be a trap.

Chapter 20:

  • Two days from St. Deborah, the Symphony came onto a resort town that had burned down to the ground: completely overgrown with wildlife, including pink flowers. They shot two deer for food and grazed their horses at a nearby baseball field.

  • Kirsten, Jackson, and Viola toured a school that was deserted, with all the evidence of what had once taken place there. That included a gym used for shelter and even a skeleton in the bathroom. They picked up a jar of rosin and a flute mouthpiece but found mostly nothingness. Kirsten returned to the Symphony to belittle what they found, particularly the skeleton in preparation for their departure.

Chapter 21:

  • François Diallo asks Kirsten Raymonde in an interview to speak about her childhood with the Georgia Flu and about how the world has changed since then. She doesn't answer. He asks her about the tattoos of knives. She won't explain and tells him he should know better than to ask.
