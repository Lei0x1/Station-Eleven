About Station Eleven

Station Eleven is a novel by Canadian author Emily St. John Mandel. Set before and after a fictional pandemic known as the "Georgia Flu," the story explores themes of survival, loss, and the power of art in a world drastically changed by the collapse of civilization. Published in 2014, the novel won the prestigious Arthur C. Clarke Award and received widespread critical acclaim, becoming a bestseller with over 1.5 million copies sold by 2020.


A ten-part television adaptation premiered on HBO Max in 2021, bringing Mandel's vision to new audiences. In 2023, Station Eleven was also a featured book on Canada Reads, championed by actor Michael Greyeyes.

This website is a tribute to the world of Station Eleven, highlighting key chapters, character moments, and themes from the novel.
