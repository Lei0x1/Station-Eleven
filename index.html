Station Eleven

by Emily St. John Mandel

Chapter 1:

  • In Act 4 of King Lear at the Elgin Theatre, actor Arthur Leander collapses on stage. Audience member Jeevan, a trainee paramedic, rushes to help him. He starts CPR with a cardiologist, but Arthur can’t be revived.

  • Jeevan comforts a young girl named Kirsten and decides to find her guardian. As he leaves, he feels guilty for surviving. Walking home in the snow, he realizes he wants to be a paramedic.

Chapter 2:

  • At the Elgin Theatre, only a few people were left. A woman washed costumes, a man ironed, and an actress drank tequila. A stagehand mopped the floor, while a caregiver comforted a crying child.

  • In the lobby, they talked about Arthur’s family. They learned he had a son named Tyler in Israel and several ex-wives. They decided to call his lawyer about his death.

  • They raised their glasses to Arthur before leaving into the snow. In the dressing room, Tanya gave Kirsten a paperweight with a storm cloud inside. The bartender was the last to leave and died three weeks later.

Chapter 3:

  • Jeevan walked in Allan Gardens, feeling the warmth of the greenhouse while snow fell outside. He admired the plants and thought about visiting his brother Frank to share the news of Arthur’s death.

  • After leaving, he took a streetcar home, remembering Arthur and his friend Hua, who warned him about the Georgia Flu spreading fast.

  • Worried, Jeevan decided to leave the city. He rushed to a grocery store for supplies and tried to call his girlfriend, Laura, but she dismissed his fears. He urged her to leave before it was too late.

Chapter 4:

  • The Elgin Theatre was empty except for a security guard and the producer, who called Arthur’s lawyer to share the news of his death. The lawyer spent two hours reviewing Arthur’s will and then contacted Arthur’s close friend, who started calling Arthur’s ex-wives the next morning.

Chapter 5:

  • While in Malaysia for work, Miranda got a call that Arthur, a man she once loved, had died from a heart attack on stage during a play. She felt lonely looking at the lit-up ships in the distance. The news came in an ordinary way, leaving her to process the loss and remember that life goes on.

Chapter 6:

  • Many everyday things are lost. No swimming, night games, or cities. Movies and concerts are rare, and there are no medicines. Air travel is mostly gone, with planes used for storage. Borders and emergency services no longer exist. The internet is gone, so people can’t connect online anymore.

Chapter 7:

  • Twenty years after air travel ended, the Traveling Symphony moved slowly through the heat near Lake Michigan. They walked to rest the horses and practiced lines from King Lear.

  • Kirsten remembered Arthur Leander, who had given her two special comics. She and August discussed their past and their interest in old technology. The Symphony continued to perform music and Shakespeare as they traveled.

Chapter 8:

  • Kirsten memorized two rare comics, Dr. Eleven, given by Arthur Leander. They follow Dr. Eleven, a physicist on a beautiful space station. Only ten copies exist, and they are worn out. One image shows him with a strange dog named Luli, thinking about Earth.

Chapter 9:

  • The Symphony arrives in St. Deborah, where they left Charlie, who was pregnant. A guard directs them to camp at Walmart, but the town feels empty. Kirsten worries about Charlie while rehearsing A Midsummer Night’s Dream with her ex, Sayid.

Chapter 10:

  • The Traveling Symphony faces conflicts among its members, but their love for music keeps them together. Kirsten searches for her friend Charlie but finds out Charlie has left with her family. She sees grave markers but learns they escaped. The Symphony hears about an epidemic and changes in town leadership.

Chapter 11:

  • In a post-apocalyptic town, Kirsten performs as Titania in A Midsummer Night’s Dream. The Traveling Symphony values art and carries the motto “Because survival is not enough.”

Chapter 12:

  • After Kirsten’s show, a man known as the prophet praises the Symphony and warns about future dangers from the flu. The conductor hears about new rules for leaving town. As they leave, the group talks about the risks ahead while Kirsten thinks about memories from the past.

Chapter 13:

  • In a Toronto restaurant, actor Arthur Leander meets Miranda, a young woman leaving her boyfriend. He offers her a place to stay, sparking questions about their relationship.

  • As Arthur thinks about his fame, he reconnects with Miranda, now an art student, showing their different lives and true connection.

Chapter 14:

  • Miranda, an admin assistant at Neptune Logistics, feels out of place next to stylish Thea. Frustrated with her boyfriend Pablo, she meets Arthur for dinner. They reconnect, and she decides to break up with Pablo, leading to a romantic moment.

Chapter 15:

  • Miranda is upset about her failing marriage to Arthur after seeing him with another woman, Elizabeth. She considers leaving but faces her feelings instead.

  • Later, she feels out of place at home and sees Elizabeth drunk. A photographer makes her feel vulnerable, but she connects with Elizabeth and they talk. Miranda starts to accept her divorce and thinks about her future.

Chapter 16:

  • Francois Daillo interviews Kirsten Raymonde twenty-six years after Miranda and Arthur’s last dinner party. Diallo has started a newspaper and wants local stories since many people don’t travel anymore. Raymonde asks about some comics she showed him, which were a gift from Arthur Leander.
